# Direct vs Marketplace: Revenue & Customer Impact Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes how direct ordering channels compare with marketplace platforms in terms of revenue, profitability, and customer retention for restaurants.

The goal was to understand the real business impact of third-party platforms and highlight the value of owning customer relationships — a core problem that platforms like Flipdish aim to solve.

---

## Key Objectives
- Compare **Direct vs Marketplace orders**
- Analyze **Revenue vs Net Revenue (after commission)**
- Evaluate **commission impact on profitability**
- Understand **customer behavior (New vs Repeat)**
- Identify **order trends by time of day**

---

## Dashboard Highlights

### Revenue vs Profitability
- Marketplace channels generate similar revenue but significantly lower **net revenue**
- Around **12% of total revenue is lost** due to commissions

### Commission Impact
- Marketplace platforms result in substantial revenue leakage
- Direct channels retain **100% of order value**

### Customer Ownership
- Direct channels show stronger **repeat customer behavior**
- Marketplace platforms are more focused on **new customer acquisition**

### Operational Insights
- Order volumes peak during **evening and night hours**
- Marketplace channels dominate during high-demand periods
---

## Key Insight

> While marketplace platforms drive order volume, they reduce profitability and customer retention due to commission costs — highlighting the importance of direct ordering channels.
---

## Dataset
A simulated dataset was created to reflect realistic restaurant ordering behavior, including:
- Order channels (Direct vs Marketplace)
- Commission structures
- Customer types (New vs Repeat)
- Delivery time and ratings
